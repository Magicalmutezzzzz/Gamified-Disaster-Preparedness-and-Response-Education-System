Video Description

This tutorial explains how smoke reduces visibility and increases toxic inhalation risk. The instructor demonstrates staying low, covering the mouth with a damp cloth, following tactile cues (walls/handrails), checking doors for heat before opening, and moving deliberately to the nearest safe exit.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand why smoke stays higher and why low-crawling is safer.
  • Learn simple breathing protection (damp cloth) and how to check doors safely.
  • Use tactile and audible exit cues when visibility is poor.
